Our Films — 2021 to present
Feature · 42 min · Released 2021

Awakening of
the Soil

Edirne · Konya · Silivri · Eskişehir

A skeptical Thracian grain farmer visits five zero-tillage practitioners across Turkey and discovers that his tractor is the main obstacle to his soil's recovery. Every field stop adds a layer: corn growing without irrigation, earthworms in a border a machine hasn't crossed in three years, soil dark enough to crumble between two fingers.

COP 2023 · China Int'l Documentary Festival · Guardian Foundation
Episode 01 · 16 min · Released 2024

Tilya

Tilya Ekolojik Kooperatif · Istanbul

Three people sign a working contract with the land and with each other on the edge of Istanbul — and Turkey's first ecological social cooperative begins. Solidarity as infrastructure. Food justice at urban scale. A cooperative built not from manifesto but from a working organism still alive.

Postane Kültür ve Doğa · 13,000+ views
Episode 02 · ~11 min · Award Winner

Burhan

Burhan Eltan · Kaz Dağları (Ida Mountains)

A former Istanbul urbanite designs 30-square-meter regenerative micro-gardens in the Ida Mountains — proving exhausted soil can feed a family without a gram of synthetic input. The engine is BioTam Compost, applied to the soil food web like a vaccine. A structure that other people land inside and don't leave.

Sihirli Tohumlar · Turkey

Seed saving as climate adaptation — the people preserving genetic heritage that sixty years of industrial agriculture erased, one variety at a time. Hands, roof spaces, and the politics of what is permitted to grow next year.

01 · Land · Antalya

Tuğba Günal &
Birhan Erkutlu

Alakır Valley, Antalya

Two architects left Istanbul and spent 22 years defending a single Mediterranean stream from 14 hydroelectric dams. Their house is built of earth. Their front line is a water pipe. A surveillance camera watches from across the property line — and they keep going anyway. This isn't an escape story. It's what it costs to stay.

02 · Water · Muğla

Zafer
Kızılkaya

Gökova Bay, Muğla

He dove for National Geographic, then left it to save one bay. To protect Gökova he talked 22 fishing cooperatives and 60 restaurants into eating the invading lionfish — five tons of it, off the reef and onto the plate. In 2023 the Goldman Prize went to a Turkish conservationist for the first time. He's still in the water.

03 · Food · Kastamonu

Balabanağa
Çiftliği

Daday, Kastamonu

A farm older than most countries. For roughly 450 years one family has kept ancient siyez wheat in rotation, grinding it by hand at first light. They refuse to let it become a museum. Fix the roof, feed the soil, set the table for the volunteer who returns every spring — that's the work, and the clock is running.

04 · Community · Muş

Muş 100 Kadın
Arıcı

Muş Plain, Eastern Anatolia

100 hives, 100 women, one plain. In some of Anatolia's richest wildflower country, not a single woman had ever been recognised as a beekeeper in her own name — until this spring. Watch the first frame rise from a hive and you watch a power balance shift inside the house. Honey as the golden form of women's labour.

05 · Systems · Kars

Çağan
Şekercioğlu

Sarıkamış, Kars

He runs Türkiye's only working wildlife corridor on a salary split between two countries. When the state won't buy the land an animal needs to cross, he buys it himself. A wolf collared in 2019 still pings its location to a phone in Salt Lake City at 3 a.m. Snow, tracks, a satellite signal — science with a fight on its hands.

06 · Hope · Şanlıurfa

Birecik
Kelaynak

Birecik, Şanlıurfa

Every February the doors open and a bird gone from the wild everywhere else on earth lifts off the Euphrates cliffs. Every June the doors close again. 277 birds in 2026. The keepers won't call it freedom and won't call it captivity — it's the only reason the bald ibis still exists at all. The camera here isn't beauty. It's a record, and a kind of mourning.
